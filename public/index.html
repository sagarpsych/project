Statement of Consent





Project Title: A Neuropsychological Analysis of Temporal Perception Through the Lifespan

Researcher/Principal Investigator: Sagar S. Lad., MS/MA., CSP, Erlene Rosowsky, PsyD, FGSA




Purpose of the Study: The research is being conducted by Sagar S. Lad, MA/MS., CSP., at William James College in partial fulfillment of a doctoral degree in psychology. I am researching how an individual’s perceptions of day-to-day experiences that can impact everyday life. I am inviting you to participate in this research project because you have indicated that you are 18 years of age or older. This form will provide you with additional information about this study, what will be asked of you and any known risks or benefits that may come from your participation. I encourage you to read this form carefully and ask any questions you may have if you decide to participate, you will be asked to indicate with “I agree” at the bottom of the page for studies conducted online).

Procedures: In this study you will be asked to complete a brief questionnaire, a cognitive task, and a brief survey that should take no more than 15-20 minutes of your time. You will asked to first fill out general demographic information, then take part of a cognitive task, which will ask you to click “YES” or “NO,” and lastly fill out a survey of your present metacognitive feelings. You can choose to skip any questions that you do not feel comfortable answering or discontinue the questionnaire, cognitive task, and survey at any time.

Potential Risks and Discomforts: The study involves no more risk than encountered in everyday life.
Potential Benefits: Although there are no direct benefits to you, we hope that in the future, other people might benefit from this study to better help understand individual’s perceptions of day-to-day experiences that can impact everyday life.

Compensation: Should you decide to participate in this study, you will be entered into a raffle to win one of five $25 Amazon gift cards. In order to be included in this raffle at the conclusion of the survey, please send an email to sagar_lad@williamjames.edu with the Subject line: “Raffle”. The raffle entry will in no way be connected to your individual responses. If you decide to stop participation at any time, you are still eligible to email the research for a raffle entry.

Confidentiality: Throughout the study your information will be kept anonymous. All electronic files will be password protected and encrypted. Only the researcher (MA/MS., CSP), his Doctoral Project Committee (Erlene Rosowsky, PsyD, PGSA; Jason Osher, PhD; Thomas Laudate, PhD) and a HIPAA complaint transcriber will have access to your materials. If a report or article is written about this research project, your identity will be protected to the maximum extent possible. Your information may be shared with representatives of William James College or governmental authorities if you or someone else is in danger or if we are required to do so by law.

Right to Withdraw and Questions: Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You may choose not to take part at all. If you decide to participate in this research, you may stop participating at any time without consequences. You are free to skip any of the questions in the metacognitive survey that you may feel uncomfortable answering. Should you decide not to participate in this study or if you stop participating at any time, you are still eligible to email the research for a raffle entry to win one of five $25 Amazon gift cards.

Participants Rights and Study Contracts: For questions or comments about this research, please contact the primary investigator (Sagar S. Lad, MS/MA., CSP at sagar_lad@williamjames.edu) and his doctoral project chair (Erlene Rosowsky, PsyD, FGSA at Erlene_rosowsky@williamjames.edu)

The research has been reviewed and approved according to the William James College IRB procedures for research involving human subjects.
